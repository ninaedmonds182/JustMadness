How It Works

Everything you need to know before the madness begins — from purchase to party.

From purchase to party — in four steps.

Here's exactly what happens, start to finish.

1

Choose your mission ⏱ 2 minutes

Browse the missions and pick the one that suits your party. Currently Dog's Bakery is available — more on the way.

2

Purchase & receive your code ⏱ Instant

Complete payment via PayPal. Your unique access code is sent to your email straight away. Each code is personal, one-use, and valid for 14 days from first activation — so you can buy ahead of the party without any rush.

3

Enter your code & set up ⏱ 10–15 min on device + 30–45 min physical prep

Visit justmadness.co.uk/welcome, enter your code and you're in. Add the children's names, choose your game difficulty levels, select your cake recipes and download your printables — all from the same device you'll use on party day. Then follow the setup guide to hide the codes and stage the challenges in the room.

⚠️
Set up on the device you will use on party day — not a different one.

Everything is saved directly on the device — names, codes, photos, game levels, recipes. There is no cloud sync, no account, no way to transfer between devices. If you do your setup on a laptop and then try to run the party on a tablet, the tablet will have nothing on it. Pick one device, use it for setup, keep it for party day. This is also by design — keeping children's data stored locally on your own device means it never passes through any server, stays entirely in your control, and is deleted the moment you clear your browser data.

4

Hand it over ⏱ Instant

Lock the party mode and give the device to the children. They're in charge now. You're their assistant — an important one, but an assistant nonetheless.

Then sit back and watch the children take over. 🐾

What happens during the mission?

A party with a beginning, a middle, and an ending they actually earned.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

Children arrive and immediately have a mission. They know what they're here to do.
A short video sets the scene — then the device goes down and the real chaos begins.
They work in teams, crack codes, run challenges and unlock each stage of the bake.

Make it even better.

One thing that consistently makes a big difference.

If you want to make the experience feel extra special from the moment children walk in — especially when guests don't all know each other — we'd suggest picking up a few items before the party:

🎨 Optional arrival activity: Decorate your apron Buy a plain white cotton apron and baker hat per child (inexpensive from most craft or kitchen shops). Lay out fabric felt pens on the table. When children arrive, they immediately get stuck into decorating their own apron and hat while waiting for others. It's a great icebreaker, it gives arriving children something purposeful to do, and it means by the time everyone's there they're already in costume and in the mood. No awkward waiting around.

You'll need: plain white cotton aprons, plain baker hats, fabric felt-tip pens. Available cheaply from craft shops, Amazon, or Hobbycraft.

A printer is preferable — it makes hiding the game codes and handing out wristbands much smoother. But if you don't have one, pen and paper works perfectly well. You can handwrite the codes onto small pieces of card and write children's names on paper strips for wristbands. It takes a few extra minutes but it works just as well.
It depends on your space and your tolerance for organised chaos. The app supports multiple teams, so there's no fixed maximum. That said, here's what we'd suggest:
  • 2–3 children per team is the sweet spot — everyone stays involved, no one gets lost in the chaos.
  • Kitchen size matters for the baking section. Can you comfortably fit 3–4 teams around the worktops? If it's tight, consider 2 teams with slightly more children each.
  • The more teams, the more mayhem — which is the point. But more than 4 teams gets logistically complex during games. Plan accordingly.
