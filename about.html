About JustMadness

The philosophy behind the madness.

Who I am

The best parties don't need an entertainer.
They need a good enough story.

I'm a mum. I've spent years planning my daughter's birthday parties — properly planning them, way more than was probably necessary.

It started with her first big party at a soft play centre. Two hours later it was over — the kids had descended like a swarm of grasshoppers and vanished just as fast, leaving nothing but crumbs and chaos. I remember looking at her afterwards thinking: I'm not sure she even knew this was her party. Nothing about it was bad, exactly. It just wasn't hers.

Looking for something better

So I went looking for something that actually felt like it belonged to her. Not another room full of borrowed activities — something with a story in it, that the kids could get properly lost in.

Everything I found was either a party company charging a fortune for something generic, or a printable pack with clues that led nowhere and called itself a treasure hunt. Neither one was it. So I started making it myself.

What the children taught me

The thing that taught me most wasn't my planning — it was watching the kids play. They'd tell me straight away, without meaning to, what was boring, what confused them, what made them forget they were even at a party because they were too busy trying to save the day. Every mission got better because of them, not because of me.

JustMadness isn't just for birthdays. Family gatherings, BBQs, school holidays, a rainy Sunday with cousins — anything where you'd rather the kids disappear into a mission than disappear behind a screen.

I built it because I couldn't find the party I wanted to give my daughter. I hope it gives your children a party worth talking about.
